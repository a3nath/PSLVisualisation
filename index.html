PSL 2K17

A cricket tournament visualisation

Pakistan Super League (PSL) is a cricket league played in Pakistan.

Cricket is a sport played between two teams in which each team gets 20 overs (120 balls) to score as many runs as possible. The team which scores the most at the end of 20 overs wins.

The game is divided into two kinds of players - batsman (person batting who hits the ball) and bowler (the person bowling who bowls the ball). A batsman's objective is to score as many runs as possbile by hiting the ball as far as possible. The further he hits the ball the more runs he scores. The bowler has an inverse objective - to stop the batsman from scoring runs by bowling such that either the batsman can't hit the ball or gets out (batsman's wicket).

The 2017 edition of PSL was played amongst 5 teams

I compared batsman performance, bowler performance and overall team performance to analyze which team had the better players.

Batting Performance

I analyzed individual batting peformances of each batsman based on the follwing criteria:

  1. Average runs: Average runs scored in each game - Higher the better
  2. Strike rate: Runs scored per 100 balls (How fast a batsman scores) - Higher the better

The plot below, compares runs scored vs the strike rate for each batsman for each team. As we scroll left to right the runs increases and bottom to top strike rate increases. The farther right and up the better. Hover on the cghart to see the values and their teams.

From the plot, we can observe that Peshawar has a heavily positively skewewd distribtuions which is not ideal as it indicates there are more batsman with low average scores. Lahore and Karachi also only have 4-5 batsman consistently scoring more than 15 runs. Quetta and Islamabd appear to be the better teams with significant number of batsmans scoring above 20 runs.

Bowling Performance

A bowlers job is to stop batsman from scoring runs. They try to either get the batsman out or minimize the number of runs they concede.

  1. Economy: Average runs conceded in an over
  2. Total Wickets Total wickets taken
  3. Bowler Variance: Consistency in number of runs given

Similar to the batsman scatter chart, the bowler scatter chart compares the bowlers on 3 metrics: As we move along the axes, number of wickets increase on the xaxis and ecoonomy increases along the y axis. The darker circle color signifies more consistency. A point on the right side but lower on the y axis with darker means the bowler takes wickets, gives away few runs and is consistent

Peshawar and Islamabad seem to dominate with good, consistent bowlers that have low economy with high total wickets. Quetta and Lahore though dont appear to have many bowlers in the low economy and high wickets section.

Like, the batsman average runs distribution, I compared bowlers economy distribution for across teams. In T20, each bowler can bowl a maximum of 4 overs so each team needs atleast 5 bolwers. Often times teams employ more than 5 bowlers to rotate their bowling and rest players. Thus, a team with lots of bolwers with good ecnomy gives them plenty of options.

Peshawar is a clear standout as it has 6 bowlers who give out less than 7 runs on average in an over. On the oter hand, Quetta only has 1 bolwer with an average less than 7.

Overall

I ranked all players and assigned each a score based on the following performance metrics. Each metric is scored on a scale of 0 to 3.

  1. Average runs scored:explain
  2. Strike rate
  3. Variance of batsman runs scored
  4. Average number of wickets taken
  5. Bowling Economy
  6. Variance of bowler runs conceded

The Player Score is calculated as the sum of all the metric scores. More explanation. Read more in methodology

To further comapre the distribution of player scores among teams, I plotted the box plots below.

Box plot paints an interesting picture. Notice that Lahore has a highest median score and infact has the best player and 11 players with score greater than 7. But the team only has 14 players. While Quetta has 19 players and 13 players with score greater than 7 and Peshawar 11 Overall, Peshawar and Quetta seem to have the most balanced teams, both of whom were the tournament finalist. Peshawar has good bowlers while Quetta has good batsman. Lahore Qalandars and Karachi Kings need to buy more players to match the other teams, specifically batsman.
