Pakistan Super League 2017 Visulisation

A cricket tournament analysis


Pakistan Super League (PSL) is a cricket league, operated by the Pakistan Cricket Board, played mostly in the Middle East.

It is the shortest format of the game in which each team has 20 overs (120 balls) to score as many runs as possible hence commonly referred to as T20.

The 2017 PSL was played amongst 5 teams

I studied how each teams players perfomed in the tournament. I broke down the performance in Batting and Bowling and assigned a score based on their batting and bowling performance. So natually, all-rounders who bat and bowl were ranked higher.



Batting Performance

In T20, batting is pretty straighforward and unlike bowling, almost every player has a batting stat.

To start, I compared individual batting peformances using the follwing 3 metrics:

  1. Average runs: Average runs scored in each game
  2. Strike rate: Runs scored per 100 balls
  3. Score variance: Batsman's scoring consistency

In the plot below, as we scroll left to right the average runs increases, bottom to top strike rate increases. The darker circle color represents more consistency.
Hover over a point and you can view the player name and team name.

There are some key takeaways from the visual, for example we can observe that Kamran Akmal, with one of the highest averge and strike rate is very inconsitent. So, in a crucial game he might not perform well.

Next, I compared the distribution of average batsman runs between teams. For each team, you can observe the number of batsman in each range.

From the plot, we can observe that Peshawar has a heavily positively skewewd distribtuions which is not ideal as it indicates there are more batsman with low average scores. Lahore and Karachi also only have 4-5 batsman consistently scoring more than 15 runs. Quetta and Islamabd appear to be the better teams with significant number of batsmans scoring above 20 runs.

Bowling Performance

A bowlers job is to stop batsman from scoring runs. They try to either get the batsman out or minimize the number of runs they concede.

Similar to the batsman scatter chart, the bowler scatter chart compares the bowlers on 3 metrics:

  1. Economy: Average runs conceded in an over
  2. Total Wickets Total wickets taken
  3. Bowler Variance: Consistency in number of runs given

As we move along the axes, number of wickets increase on the xaxis and ecoonomy increases along the y axis. The darker circle color signifies more consistency.

Peshawar and Islamabad seem to dominate with good, consistent bowlers that have low economy with high total wickets. Quetta and Lahore though dont appear to have many bowlers in the low economy and high wickets section.

Like, the batsman average runs distribution, I compared bowlers economy distribution for across teams.
In T20, each bowler can bowl a maximum of 4 overs so each team needs atleast 5 bolwers. Often times teams employ more than 5 bowlers to rotate their bowling and rest players. Thus, a team with lots of bolwers with good ecnomy gives them plenty of options.

Peshawar is a clear standout as it has 6 bowlers who give out less than 7 runs on average in an over. Quetta can improve in the bowling department since they only have 1 bolwer with an average less than 7.

Overall

I ranked all players and assigned each a score based on the following performance metrics. Each metric is scored on a scale of 0 to 3.

  1. Average runs scored
  2. Strike rate
  3. Variance of batsman runs scored
  4. Average number of wickets taken
  5. Bowling Economy
  6. Variance of bowler runs conceded

The Player Score is calculated as the sum of all the metric scores.
Naturally, all-rounders, who can bat as well as bowl, contribute most to a team and thus rank higher.

Data and Methodology

I was inspired by this Kaggle project which is a similar analysis but with Indian Premier League data.
I used ball-ball data for 24 matches (48 innings) where each data point includes delivery number, bowler and batsman name and the outcome of the delivery.
